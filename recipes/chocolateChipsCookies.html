Chocolate Chips Cookies Recipe

Image from Bakes by Brown Sugar

Description

Who want cookies? With this recipi you will be able to make the best-looking cookies ever. features chocolate chips or chocolate morsels as its distinguishing ingredient. Chocolate chip cookies are claimed to have originated in the United States in 1938, when Ruth Graves Wakefield chopped up a Nestlé semi-sweet chocolate bar and added the chopped chocolate to a cookie recipe; however, historical recipes for grated or chopped chocolate cookies exist prior to 1938 by various other authors. Making bakery-worthy chocolate chip cookies is much easier than it seems.

Ingredients

For 1

  • 1 cup butter
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 1 cup Item
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons hot water
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips
  • Item Item
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Beat butter, white sugar, and brown sugar in a large bowl with an electric mixer until smooth and creamy.
  2. Beat in eggs, one at a time, then stir in vanilla.
  3. Dissolve baking soda in hot water; add to batter along with salt and mix until combined.
  4. Stir in flour, chocolate chips, and walnuts until a soft dough forms.
  5. Drop rounded spoonfuls of cookie dough 2 inches apart onto ungreased baking sheets.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven until edges are lightly browned, about 10 minutes.
  7. Cool on the baking sheets briefly before removing to a wire rack to cool completely.
  8. Store in an airtight container or serve immediately.
  9. Enjoy!
